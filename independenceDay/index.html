Journey to Freedom

1857

First War of Independence

The Indian Rebellion of 1857 was a major uprising against the rule of the British East India Company. Though unsuccessful, it marked the beginning of the organized struggle for independence.

1885

Formation of Indian National Congress

The Indian National Congress was founded, becoming the primary organization leading the independence movement through peaceful and constitutional means.

1919

Jallianwala Bagh Massacre

British troops fired on a large crowd of unarmed Indians in Amritsar, killing hundreds. This event galvanized the independence movement.

1930

Salt March

Mahatma Gandhi led the Dandi Salt March, a 24-day non-violent protest against the British salt monopoly. This became a pivotal event in the civil disobedience movement.

1942

Quit India Movement

Mahatma Gandhi launched the Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule. The movement gained massive public support across the country.

1947

Independence Achieved

After decades of struggle, India finally gained independence on August 15, 1947. Jawaharlal Nehru became the first Prime Minister of independent India.
